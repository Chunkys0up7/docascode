## Introduction

This guide outlines our organization's approach to compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which governs the processing of personal data of individuals in the European Union.

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All processing of personal data within the EU
- Processing of personal data of EU residents, regardless of processing location
- All employees, contractors, and third-party processors

## Key Principles

### 1. Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency

Personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner.

**Legal Bases for Processing:**
- Consent
- Contract performance
- Legal obligation
- Vital interests
- Public task
- Legitimate interests

### 2. Purpose Limitation

Data must be collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes.

### 3. Data Minimization

Collection must be adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes.

### 4. Accuracy

Personal data must be accurate and kept up to date. Inaccurate data must be erased or rectified without delay.

### 5. Storage Limitation

Data must be kept in a form that permits identification of data subjects for no longer than necessary.

**Retention Periods:**
- Customer data: 7 years after last transaction
- Employee records: 7 years after termination
- Marketing data: Until consent is withdrawn
- Application logs: 90 days

### 6. Integrity and Confidentiality

Data must be processed securely using appropriate technical and organizational measures.

## Data Subject Rights

### Right to Access (Article 15)

Data subjects can request:
- Confirmation of data processing
- Copy of personal data
- Information about processing purposes
- Information about data recipients

**Response Time:** Within 30 days

### Right to Rectification (Article 16)

Data subjects can request correction of inaccurate personal data.

### Right to Erasure (Article 17)

Also known as "right to be forgotten." Applies when:
- Data no longer necessary
- Consent withdrawn
- Data unlawfully processed
- Legal obligation requires erasure

**Exceptions:**
- Legal claims
- Public interest
- Legal obligations

### Right to Restriction (Article 18)

Data subjects can request restriction of processing when:
- Accuracy is contested
- Processing is unlawful
- Data no longer needed but required for legal claims

### Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

Data subjects can receive their personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

### Right to Object (Article 21)

Data subjects can object to:
- Processing based on legitimate interests
- Direct marketing
- Scientific/historical research

## Data Protection by Design and Default

### Privacy by Design

Implement technical and organizational measures to ensure data protection principles are integrated into processing activities.

**Measures:**
- Pseudonymization and encryption
- Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality
- Ability to restore data availability
- Regular testing and evaluation

### Privacy by Default

Ensure that by default, only personal data necessary for each purpose is processed.

**Default Settings:**
- Minimal data collection
- Limited processing scope
- Short retention periods
- Limited accessibility

## Data Breach Response

### Detection and Assessment

Monitor systems for potential breaches and assess:
- Nature of breach
- Categories of data affected
- Number of data subjects affected
- Potential consequences

### Notification Requirements

#### To Supervisory Authority
- Within 72 hours of becoming aware
- Required unless unlikely to result in risk

#### To Data Subjects
- Without undue delay
- Required if likely to result in high risk
- Clear and plain language

### Breach Response Plan

1. **Contain:** Isolate affected systems
2. **Assess:** Determine scope and impact
3. **Notify:** Contact DPO and legal team
4. **Investigate:** Root cause analysis
5. **Document:** Maintain breach register
6. **Report:** Notify authorities and affected individuals
7. **Review:** Update security measures

## Third-Party Processors

### Due Diligence

Assess processors for:
- Technical and organizational measures
- Security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- Previous data breaches
- Sub-processor arrangements

### Data Processing Agreements (DPA)

Required elements:
- Subject matter and duration
- Nature and purpose of processing
- Type of personal data
- Categories of data subjects
- Processor obligations
- Security measures
- Sub-processor terms
- Data subject rights support
- Audit rights

### Approved Processors

Maintain list of approved processors with:
- Processor name and contact
- Services provided
- DPA execution date
- Last security review
- Compliance status

## International Data Transfers

### Transfer Mechanisms

For transfers outside EU/EEA:
- **Adequacy Decisions:** Countries with adequate protection
- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):** EU-approved contracts
- **Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs):** Internal company rules
- **Explicit Consent:** For specific transfers

### Transfer Impact Assessment

Assess:
- Legal regime in destination country
- Safeguards in place
- Risk to data subjects
- Additional measures needed

## Employee Training

### Mandatory Training

All employees must complete:
- GDPR fundamentals (annually)
- Data handling procedures (annually)
- Breach response (annually)

### Role-Specific Training

Additional training for:
- **Developers:** Privacy by design, secure coding
- **Marketers:** Consent management, opt-outs
- **HR:** Employee data handling
- **Support:** Data subject rights requests

## Compliance Monitoring

### Internal Audits

- Quarterly data processing reviews
- Annual compliance audits
- Regular security assessments

### Key Metrics

- Data subject requests processed
- Average response time
- Breaches identified and remediated
- Training completion rates
- Processor compliance reviews

### Documentation

Maintain records of:
- Processing activities
- Data protection impact assessments
- Data subject requests and responses
- Data breaches
- Training completion

## Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

### When Required

Conduct DPIA for processing that is likely to result in high risk:
- Systematic and extensive profiling
- Large-scale processing of special categories
- Systematic monitoring of public areas

### DPIA Process

1. Describe processing operation
2. Assess necessity and proportionality
3. Identify risks to data subjects
4. Identify measures to address risks
5. Document and review
