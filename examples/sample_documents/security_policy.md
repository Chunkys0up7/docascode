# Information Security Policy

**Version:** 2.1
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025
**Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer

## 1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting the organization's information assets, ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties who have access to the organization's information systems and data.

## 3. Password Requirements

### 3.1 Complexity

All passwords must meet the following requirements:
- Minimum length of 12 characters
- Include uppercase and lowercase letters
- Include at least one number
- Include at least one special character
- Cannot contain dictionary words or personal information

### 3.2 Rotation

- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- Cannot reuse last 5 passwords
- Temporary passwords must be changed on first login

## 4. Access Control

### 4.1 Principle of Least Privilege

Users shall be granted only the minimum access necessary to perform their job functions.

### 4.2 Access Review

- Quarterly reviews of user access rights
- Immediate revocation upon termination
- Annual recertification of privileged accounts

## 5. Data Classification

### 5.1 Public
Information that can be freely shared externally.

### 5.2 Internal
Information intended for internal use only.

### 5.3 Confidential
Sensitive information requiring protection from unauthorized disclosure.

### 5.4 Restricted
Highly sensitive information with strict access controls and regulatory requirements.

## 6. Incident Response

All security incidents must be reported to the Security Operations Center (SOC) within 1 hour of discovery.

### 6.1 Severity Levels

- **Critical**: Immediate threat to business operations
- **High**: Significant security breach with potential data loss
- **Medium**: Security event requiring investigation
- **Low**: Minor security violation

## 7. Compliance

This policy supports compliance with:
- SOC 2 Type II
- ISO 27001
- GDPR
- HIPAA (where applicable)

## 8. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and potential legal action.

## 9. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed and updated annually or as needed to address emerging threats and regulatory changes.
